The article examines SBTi 2.0's implications for carbon credit markets, arguing meaningful near-term demand for reductions and nature-based removals will emerge through the Ongoing Emissions Responsibility program while durable CDR waits for the 2030s. The framework creates three recognition tiers (Engaged, Advanced, Leadership) requiring companies to offset 1-100% of emissions with credits, but engineered carbon removal won't see significant demand until 2035 when mandatory removal credit purchases begin. It suggests this benefits nature-based and reduction credits priced around $20-80 per tonne.
